## Does Apple Cider Vinegar Help Gut Health?

To make apple cider vinegar, farmers ferment crushed apples until
the natural sugars turn into acetic acid. That acetic acid does all

When you shop, look for the raw, unfiltered kind. Floating in the

Clear apple cider vinegar from the grocery shelf is a different
product altogether. During processing, manufacturers strip out the

raw and unfiltered.

The difference between raw and processed apple cider vinegar is
not subtle. Raw apple cider vinegar carries a sharp, earthy smell
and a cloudy appearance that tells you the mother is still alive
and working. Processed apple cider vinegar looks clean and clear
in the bottle but that clarity comes at a cost. Once manufacturers
filter out the mother, the enzymes, the live cultures, and the
acetic acid concentration all lose their potency. You are paying

## A Few Notes Before You Pour Your First Glass

For the majority of people, apple cider vinegar is safe and gentle.
A few notes are worth knowing before your first glass.

Always dilute it in water first. Drinking it straight wears on your
tooth enamel and irritates the throat over time. Warm water
protects both.

On blood sugar medication? Talk to your doctor before adding apple
cider vinegar to your morning. It affects blood sugar levels, and
you want it working with your medication, not against it.

For a sensitive stomach, pour one teaspoon into your water instead
of a full tablespoon. Over one to two weeks, work up to the full
amount and give your body time to adjust without rushing it.

One more thing worth knowing. Store your apple cider vinegar away
from heat and direct light. The mother is alive and heat breaks it
down over time. A cool dark cabinet keeps the bottle potent for
far beyond a year. Shake the bottle with care before each use so
the mother distributes without interruption through the liquid
before you pour.
