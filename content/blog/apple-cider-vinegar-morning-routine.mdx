## What Is Apple Cider Vinegar, Anyway?

To make apple cider vinegar, farmers ferment crushed apples until
the natural sugars turn into acetic acid. That acetic acid does all
the work.

When you shop, look for the raw, unfiltered kind. Floating in the
bottle you will see something cloudy and dark. People call that
layer the mother. Inside it live natural enzymes and the good
bacteria your gut craves. Never shake it out — that is the good
stuff.

Clear apple cider vinegar from the grocery shelf is a different
product altogether. During processing, manufacturers strip out the
mother and the benefits disappear along with it. Always choose
raw and unfiltered.

## What It Does for Your Gut

To digest food properly, your stomach must stay acidic. When stomach
acid drops low, food sits there instead of breaking down. Bloating,
gas, and that heavy feeling after meals all follow from that one problem.

Every morning, apple cider vinegar brings that acidity back to where it
belongs. Within a few days of adding it to their routine, people notice
bloating ease up and digestion smooth out. That quick response is the
acid signaling the stomach to wake up and do its job.

For your gut bacteria, the natural enzymes in raw apple cider vinegar
act as food for the good bacteria that need to stay in charge. A
balanced gut means a calmer belly all day long.

## How I Take It Every Morning

Simple is the way I have always done it. Here is the whole recipe.

Pour one tablespoon of raw apple cider vinegar into eight ounces of
warm water. Add a little raw honey if you want it sweeter. Stir it
up and drink it slow before you eat anything.

## A Few Notes Before You Pour Your First Glass

For nearly everyone, apple cider vinegar is safe and gentle. A few notes
are worth knowing before your first glass.

Always dilute it in water first. Drinking it straight wears on your tooth
enamel and irritates the throat over time. Warm water protects both.

On blood sugar medication? Talk to your doctor before adding apple cider
vinegar to your morning. It affects blood sugar levels, and you want it
working with your medication, not against it.

For a sensitive stomach, pour one teaspoon into your water instead of a
full tablespoon. Over one to two weeks, work up to the full amount and
give your body time to adjust without rushing it.
