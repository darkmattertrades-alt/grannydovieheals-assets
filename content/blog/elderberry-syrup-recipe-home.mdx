## How to Make Elderberry Syrup at Home

This is the recipe Granny Dovie trusts. Four simple
ingredients. About 45 minutes from first step to finish.
You do not need special equipment. Only a pot, a
strainer, and a clean glass jar.

### What You Need

- 1 cup dried black elderberries
- 3 cups cold filtered water
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger
- 1 cup raw honey added after cooling

### How to Make It

Pour the elderberries and cold water into a medium
saucepan. Add the cinnamon stick and grated ginger.
Bring to a boil over medium heat. Then reduce to a
low simmer.

Let it simmer uncovered for 45 minutes. The liquid
will reduce by about half. The berries will soften.
They will release their deep purple color into the
water.

Remove the pan from the heat. Let it cool to room
temperature. Never add honey to hot liquid. Heat
above 95 degrees kills the natural enzymes and
bacteria-fighting compounds in raw honey.

Once cooled, pour through a fine mesh strainer into
a clean glass bowl. Press the berries with the back
of a spoon. Get every drop. Discard the spent berries.

Stir in one cup of raw honey. Let it dissolve all the
way into the elderberry liquid. Pour into a clean
glass jar with a tight lid.

Store in the refrigerator. Keeps for up to 60 days
when stored cold.
